About Me

Hello and welcome to my corner of the internet. I am Souvik Das, a program management professional currently working in the aerospace sector. Over the years, I have had the opportunity to work with organizations such as Airbus, Alstom and Accenture, contributing to programs and projects spanning engineering, digital services, technology and business transformation. Professionally, I enjoy bringing structure to complexity and helping teams navigate large-scale initiatives. Personally, I am driven by curiosity. I hail from Kolkata (Calcutta), a city that continues to inspire me regardless of where I live. As a Bengali, I have a deep affection for its history, culture, architecture, literature and intellectual traditions. I enjoy reading about old Calcutta, its industrial heritage, its transformation through different eras and its place in modern India. This website is an extension of those interests—a place to share thoughts, articles, travel experiences, books, conversations and reflections on the world around us.

Professional Journey

My career has taken me across multiple industries and geographies. I have worked with Accenture, Alstom and Airbus, primarily in program and project management roles. My experience spans digital services, engineering environments, transformation programs, stakeholder management and large-scale delivery initiatives. What I enjoy most is working at the intersection of people, technology and business outcomes.

Interests & Curiosities

Outside work, I enjoy exploring a broad range of subjects. Politics, economics, public policy and governance are recurring interests. I enjoy reading across ideological traditions—from Marxist writings and the Communist Manifesto to conservative, liberal and nationalist schools of thought. Understanding different perspectives is often more interesting than defending a single one. I generally believe societies progress through a combination of political will, capable institutions, economic opportunity, good governance and human-centered development. I consider myself broadly liberal in outlook while also appreciating the role of traditions and cultural continuity. I am a believer in free markets, globalization and open exchange of ideas, while remaining interested in how societies can balance growth with social well-being. Vedanta and Indian philosophical traditions are another area of interest. I enjoy exploring questions about ethics, meaning, consciousness and the human experience. I am also a lifelong cricket enthusiast and an admirer of sport as a reflection of culture, competition and human aspiration. Books occupy a significant portion of my shelves, though perhaps not always enough of my time. Detective fiction remains a favourite genre, alongside history, economics, politics and philosophy.

Travel & Food

Travel remains one of my favourite ways to understand people and cultures. Europe is my preferred region to explore and London remains one of my favourite cities. I enjoy discovering museums, bookstores, cafés, historical neighbourhoods and public spaces that reveal the story of a place. Food is another passion. My preferences remain firmly rooted in Bengali cuisine, though I am equally fond of Chinese food and the uniquely iconic Kolkata biryani. I firmly believe that one of the best ways to understand a culture is through its food, conversations and everyday life.
